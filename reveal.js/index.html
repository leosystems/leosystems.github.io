Emergence of LEO systems

  • Lower propagation delay in LEO vs GEO
    • Toll quality voice circuits now possible
    • IP over satellite
  • Use of Ka frequency band vs C and Ku
    • Smaller antennas
  • Lower launch costs
    • Multiple satellites per launch
    • New launch sites in China and Russia
  • Bandwidth demand

Characteristics of LEO systems

  • "Anytime, anywhere"
  • Blends cellular and satellite technologies
    • Satellite
    • Terrestrial
    • Gateways
  • 500-2000 km
  • Fiber-like propagation delay

LEO versus GEO

  • Less propagation delay for LEOs
  • More LEOs required for global coverage
  • Single LEO failure will not result in lost communication
  • Less exposure to Van Allen Belt radiation
  • Inter-satellite links versus bent-pipe
  • Jitter

Iridium

  • Mobile Uplink/Downlink (MHz): 1616-1626.5 (L-band)
  • Orbit altitude: 780 km
  • Number of orbit planes: 6
  • Company: Motorola

Globalstar

  • Mobile Uplink/Downlink (MHz): 1616-1626.5 (L-band)
  • Orbit altitude: 1410 km
  • Number of orbit planes: 8
  • Company: Loral, Alcatel, Qualcomm

Constellation Communications

  • Mobile Uplink/Downlink (MHz): 2483.5-2500 (L-band)
  • Orbit altitude: 2000 km
  • Number of orbit planes: 8
  • Orbital Sciences, Bell Atlantic, Raytheon

Government involvement

  • Enhanced Mobile Satellite Service (EMSS)
    • Iridium network
    • 4 DISA contracts (> $300 M)
    • Secure calling via DoD gateway
    • End-to-end security via secure phones
  • Future Narrow Band Digital Terminal (FNBDT)
    • Government funded / Industry supported
    • Working Group formed to develop secure mode of operation between STU-III and GSM, CDMA and LEO satellite systems such as Iridium and Globalstar
